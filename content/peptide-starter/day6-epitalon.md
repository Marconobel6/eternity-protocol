## 2. What the science actually says

- Human evidence is limited but suggests Epitalon may support melatonin production, normalize cortisol rhythms, and influence telomere biology through telomerase/ALT pathways.[1][2]
- 2025 cell-line studies showed telomere lengthening in periodontal stem cells, but doses were tiny and context-specific—nothing like “10 mg daily” internet claims.[2]
- Sleep improvements often stem from pineal support + circadian gene regulation (CLOCK, CRY2, CSNK1E) rather than direct telomere effects.[2]

## 3. Practical routine

| Step | Details |
| --- | --- |
| Reconstitution | Add 0.5 mL bac water to 10 mg vial (20 mg/mL). Roll gently, no shaking.|
| Dosing | 1 mg (0.05 mL) subcutaneous every morning for 10–20 days. Microcycle 1–2 times per year.|
| Storage | 2–8 °C, back of fridge to minimize vibration, light-protective sleeve.|
| Pairings | Morning sunlight within 10 minutes of waking, consistent wake time, evening glycine (10 g) + 4 PM hydration cutoff (from Jorn’s protocol) to reinforce circadian cues.* |

Avoid “mega dosing” (5–10 mg daily) until human data exists; overstimulation can theoretically suppress melatonin.[2]

## 4. Tracking impact

- **Sleep data:** WHOOP/Oura deep sleep minutes, sleep latency, and mid-night awakenings.
- **Hormones:** Melatonin saliva test (if available) pre/post cycle; cortisol AM/PM ratio.
- **Subjective:** Morning energy, mood, nocturia frequency (Jorn’s hydration protocol aims to reduce it).
- **Advanced:** Telomere testing (e.g., TruDiagnostic) only if you’re okay with noise; it’s expensive and slow.
