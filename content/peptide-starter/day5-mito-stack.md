> TL;DR: SLU-PP-332 isn’t a peptide at all—it’s a small molecule ERR agonist that mimics aerobic training. MOTS-c is a mitochondrial-derived peptide that activates AMPK. Together they form the “metabolic flexibility” combo mentioned in the WhatsApp stack, but all evidence remains preclinical. Treat it like Tier 3 experimentation.

## 1. WhatsApp context

The February 6 message mapped SLU-PP-332 Monday–Friday (100–300 mcg oral) with MOTS-c 500 mcg on weekends for energy.* That’s a popular “mitochondrial stack” in longevity circles. We translate it into a transparent protocol.

## 2. Mechanisms (keep it simple for readers)

| Compound | Type | Mechanism | Rodent outcomes |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| **SLU-PP-332** | Small molecule | Activates estrogen-related receptors (ERRα/β/γ) → upregulates PGC-1α → mitochondrial biogenesis + fat oxidation | +25% fat oxidation, more type IIa fibers, higher endurance without extra exercise, better glucose tolerance.[1][3]
| **MOTS-c** | 16-aa peptide | Activates AMPK → improved glucose uptake, insulin sensitivity, and mitochondrial efficiency | Better metabolic health markers, stress resistance, fat burning in mice.[1][2]

Sources like Derek Pruski’s 2025 “Ultimate Mito Stack” stress layering: structural protection (SS-31), metabolic signaling (MOTS-c), gene activation (SLU-PP-332), and electron transport support (Methylene Blue).[1]

## 3. Experimental protocol (research use only)

| Day | Compound | Suggested range | Notes |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Mon–Fri | SLU-PP-332 | 50–100 mg oral capsule (doses vary widely due to limited data) | Morning with light food to avoid nausea; cycle 8–10 weeks.[1][2]
| Sat–Sun | MOTS-c | 200–500 mcg subcutaneous | Morning doses to capitalize on weekend training; some split Sat/Sun at 500 mcg each.[2]

Because human data is nonexistent, recommend microdosing (e.g., 50 mg SLU) and lab monitoring (fasting glucose, insulin, lipids) every 4 weeks.

## 4. Measurement framework

- **Metabolic panel:** Fasting glucose/insulin, HOMA-IR every 4 weeks.
- **DEXA or MRI:** Track VAT shifts after 8–12 weeks.
- **VO2 max test:** Pre- and post-cycle to validate endurance claims.
- **Mito markers:** If possible, run metabolomics or lactate testing to document substrate use.

## 5. Safety + sourcing

- SLU-PP-332 is not approved for human use anywhere; sold as “research chemical.”
- MOTS-c also lacks FDA approval; treat like any injectable peptide (COA, sterile technique, refrigeration).
- People with thyroid issues, arrhythmias, or metabolic disorders should only proceed under medical supervision.
