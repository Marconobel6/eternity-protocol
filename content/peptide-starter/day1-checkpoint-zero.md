> TL;DR: Peptides move faster than your diagnostics. The safest beginners spend Day 1 collecting data—labs, imaging, sleep metrics, and sourcing docs—so every later adjustment is anchored to objective baselines.

## 1. Start with a real assessment, not vibes

The most respected voices in Marco's WhatsApp longevity groups start every peptide conversation with diagnostics. In the February 6 coaching drop, the stack outline literally opened with: “Our standard before we put clients on peptides is to do full body MRI, blood panel, and toxin screening.”* That discipline keeps the shiny objects grounded.

**Non‑negotiable screens before Day 2:**

| Bucket | Why it matters | Minimum tests |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **Metabolic Panel** | Growth hormone secretagogues swing glucose/insulin. Catch dysregulation before Sermorelin. | Fasting glucose, HbA1c, fasting insulin, CMP with liver enzymes, lipid panel, hs-CRP.[1][2]
| **Hormones** | Baseline IGF‑1, thyroid, sex hormones guide dosing and help interpret results later. | IGF‑1, cortisol (AM), TSH, free T4, total/free testosterone, estradiol, LH/FSH, SHBG.[1][2]
| **Hematology** | Tracks systemic inflammation and oxygen-carrying capacity. | CBC with differential, ferritin.[1]
| **Kidney/Liver** | Most peptides clear through liver/kidneys; compromised clearance increases risk. | CMP (creatinine/eGFR), urinalysis, AST/ALT/ALP/bilirubin.[1]
| **Body Composition** | You need a map for visceral fat and lean tissue. | DEXA or MRI for VAT + lean mass; at minimum, InBody every 30 days.|
| **Sleep/Circadian** | Later peptides target sleep depth; capture baselines now. | WHOOP/Oura data export, 7-day sleep log, caffeine/alcohol timing review (e.g., 4 PM cutoff from Jorn’s note).* |

*WhatsApp Longevity Circle, Feb 6 2026.

## 2. Layer imaging + functional tests

Peptide stacks promise cartilage repair and visceral fat cuts—prove it. Recommended day-one imaging/tests:

- **Full-body MRI or DEXA** for VAT and organ screening (referenced directly in the WhatsApp stack).*
- **VO2 max or lactate threshold** test if you plan to deploy mitochondrial stacks (SLU‑PP‑332 + MOTS-c) later; otherwise you’ll never know if “energy” claims are real.
- **Orthopedic baselines** (ultrasound or MRI) for any tendon/joint you plan to treat with BPC-157/TB-500.

## 3. Build a sourcing + compliance folder

Set up a shared, access-controlled folder with:

1. **Lab PDFs + imaging reports** (dated).
2. **Signed medical clearance** if you’re working with a clinician (strongly recommended).
3. **Product COAs** from every supplier. No COA, no peptide.
4. **Needle/Nasal SOPs**: mixing instructions, storage temps, disposal notes.
5. **Contraindication matrix**: list current medications, allergies, and red flags (e.g., uncontrolled diabetes = pause Sermorelin until managed).

This folder becomes your audit trail for regulators, investors, and community members asking “how serious is Eternity about safety?”

## 4. Decide your entry point (injectable vs. needle-free)

Day 1 isn’t about choosing peptides—but you should log which category you’ll test first:

- **Injectable growth-hormone axis** (e.g., Sermorelin 100–300 mcg nightly). Backed by wellness clinics recommending pre-bed injections 30–60 minutes after the last meal to sync with natural GH pulses.[3][4]
- **Topical/Intranasal low-friction start** (Semax, Selank, GHK-Cu, SNAP-8). Research chemicals only; note legal status and start with low doses.[5]
- **Recovery stack** (Cartalax, BPC-157, TB-500). Evidence is largely preclinical, so your diagnostics + subjective scoring matter more than marketing claims.[6][7]

Document your planned path, the biomarkers you’ll track, and the cadence for lab re-tests (usually every 12 weeks for GH-axis peptides, sooner if glucose shifts).[1]

## 5. Pre-build your tracking dashboard

Use Notion, Sheets, or Supabase to capture:

- **Protocol timeline** (start/stop dates, taper notes).
- **Daily adherence + subjective score** (sleep quality, soreness, mood, cravings).
- **Objective markers** (resting HRV, fasting glucose, morning weight, waist circumference).
- **Safety triggers** (e.g., IGF‑1 > 350 ng/mL, ALT/AST >2x baseline, resting HR up >10 bpm for a week).
