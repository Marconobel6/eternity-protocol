## 2. Tesamorelin – the VAT assassin

- **Mechanism:** GHRH analog approved for HIV lipodystrophy; stimulates pituitary GH release specifically targeting visceral fat.[1]
- **Evidence:** Clinical trials show **15–20% VAT reduction over 26 weeks**, triglycerides down ~12%, liver fat down up to 37%, minimal subcutaneous fat loss, and improved markers like CRP.[1][3]
- **Standard wellness protocol:** 1–2 mg subcutaneous, nightly, ideally 60 minutes before bed away from food.[3]

## 3. Ipamorelin – recovery & sleep support

- **Mechanism:** GHRP (growth hormone releasing peptide) with selective GH release and minimal cortisol/prolactin impact. Enhances muscle repair, fat metabolism, and sleep quality via gentle GH pulses.[2]
- **Protocol:** 200–300 mcg subcutaneous upon waking, 5 days/week. Some run a second dose pre-bed, but we keep the simple morning slot to avoid GH overlap.[2]

## 5. Measurement framework

- **Lab panel every 6 weeks:** IGF‑1, fasting glucose/insulin, HbA1c, lipids, liver enzymes.
- **Body composition:** DEXA or MRI to quantify VAT (baseline, week 8, week 16).
- **Sleep metrics:** WHOOP/Oura deep sleep minutes; aim for +15–20 minutes like Jorn projected.
- **Subjective:** Appetite, pump quality (tesamorelin often improves gym pumps, per Jorn’s tadalafil aside), waist circumference.

## 6. Risk + logistics

- Tesamorelin is a prescription drug; requires clinician oversight and can be expensive. Sourcing via compounding pharmacies or legitimate suppliers only.
- Monitor for injection site reactions, water retention, or carpal tunnel-like symptoms; reduce dose if they appear.
- Contraindicated in active malignancies and uncontrolled diabetes.
- Combine with lifestyle: evening alcohol cutoff (4 PM), high-protein meals, resistance training 3x/week, zone 2 cardio 2x/week to accelerate VAT loss.
