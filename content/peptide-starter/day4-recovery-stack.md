> TL;DR: Treat the “recovery stack” like a research protocol. Cartalax shows promising preclinical data for cartilage integrity; BPC-157 and TB-500 (the “Wolverine stack”) are anecdote-heavy but popular. Keep cycles short, use imaging plus performance tests, and log everything as if you’ll publish it.

## 2. Cartalax – cartilage bioregulator

- **Mechanism:** Synthetic tripeptide derived from type XI collagen; targets chondrocyte proliferation, ECM preservation, and gene expression (downregulates p16/p21/p53, upregulates SIRT6).[1][4]
- **Evidence:** No human efficacy trials yet. Russian clinics report tolerability; lab/animal data show improved cartilage resilience and reduced inflammatory markers.[1][2]
- **Research dosing references:** Subcutaneous 2–5 mg daily for 10–20 days, repeated 1–3 times yearly, or 300–500 mcg post workout for localized support (per WhatsApp field note + supplier protocols).[2][3]

## 3. BPC-157 + TB-500 (“Wolverine Stack”)

- **Mechanisms:**
  - **BPC-157:** Angiogenesis, fibroblast migration, cytokine reduction—works best near injury site.[5]
  - **TB-500:** Systemic thymosin beta-4 analog promoting cell migration, actin regulation, and reduced inflammation.[5]
- **Stack protocol:**
  - BPC-157: 250–500 mcg injected near injury 1–2x/day.
  - TB-500: 2–5 mg weekly, divided into two subcutaneous injections.[5]
  - Cycle 4–6 weeks; extend only with medical oversight.
- **Evidence:** Mostly mechanistic + anecdotal; no FDA-approved dosing, no randomized human trials. Present it that way.

## 4. Experiment design for the Eternity community

| Step | Action |
| --- | --- |
| Baseline | MRI or ultrasound of the target joint/tendon, range-of-motion and strength tests, pain score, gait analysis.
| Protocol | Document compounds, suppliers, COAs, syringes/needles, mixing instructions, rotation schedule.
| Monitoring | Weekly pain + ROM scores, monthly ultrasound if possible, WHOOP strain vs. recovery for athletes.
| Safety triggers | Infection signs, unexplained edema, resting HR spikes, lab abnormalities (ALT/AST >2x baseline), or zero improvement after 6 weeks.
| Post-cycle | Repeat imaging/tests at week 8–12; publish a case study anonymized for the Eternity blog.
